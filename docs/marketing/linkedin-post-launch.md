# LinkedIn Launch Post — YIMA × Novawerk

**Title:** Shipping YIMA — the first product out of a non-profit experiment we've been quietly running from Berlin.

---

Hey everyone — long time no see. This is what I've been quietly working on for the last few months.

The pace of change in the world right now is wild. For anyone who likes building products, who actually wants to see real people use them, who wants the thing they make to do a little good — this is honestly the best moment in a generation to be working. With AI as leverage, any one person can have outsized impact. Any one person can finally build the thing they wanted to build.

Since the start of the year I've been turning over the same question: *what should I actually be making?*

I've been re-reading Hannah Arendt's *The Human Condition*. One of her points has been sitting with me. In modern society — atomised, individualised, where each of us has become a kind of island — looking after your own life is no longer enough. Good ideas can't stay inside your head. They have to appear in the world, through language and through action, and they have to resonate inside a plural, differentiated public to count for anything at all.

Look around. Every person is suddenly working at several-times their previous productivity thanks to AI. Intelligence is becoming as cheap and as ubiquitous as electricity. And yet — almost every app born from a sincere intent to make life better ends up bending, slowly, to the same business gravity. Friction is added. Paywalls go up. Decisions stop being made on usefulness and start being made on retention and conversion. Whatever was good inside the original idea gets dragged out of shape.

The result, I think, is that a lot of capable people are stuck in hesitation. I've spoken to a number of founders and full-time engineers here in Berlin who have good ideas, but who also have families, kids, jobs. A side project that has to earn money becomes heavy very fast. Because life turns out to work the same way the AI productivity boom does: just having more, on its own, doesn't make you happier — it just makes you more tired.

So a few of us wanted to try something different.

What if the apps that genuinely make life better could simply be *clean* again? What if, with AI doing the heavy lifting, anyone with a real idea — and even just five or ten minutes a day to give to it — could feel the pleasure of putting something positive into the digital world? What if we could run products on a non-profit basis, sustained by community contribution, surviving on their own terms — without a business case having to justify every design decision?

That's the experiment.

We started **Novawerk**, an open, non-profit community based in Berlin. Tagline: *Not profitable, but meaningful.*

Over the past few months we've found that, with AI doing the heavy lifting on the parts that used to require entire organisations, a small group of friends working evenings and weekends can run the full path end-to-end: iOS, Android, design, copy, translation, compliance, store submission. Things that would have been impossible at this team size even two years ago.

**YIMA** is our first attempt — and our first product.

It's a privacy-first menstrual cycle calendar:

- No accounts, no cloud, no analytics, no ads, no in-app purchases
- All data stays on your device — there is literally no backend to leak from
- Optional local sync with Apple Health / Google Health
- Reminders, exports, multiple views (notifications off by default — you turn them on if you want them)
- Fully open source on GitHub, end-to-end auditable

Available now on the **App Store**. Android version is on the way.

Built entirely by a few friends in our spare time. Run by the community. We'll update it at a deliberately unhurried, part-time pace — but we intend to keep it going for the long run.

If you have ideas, suggestions, or even just a one-line reaction, we'd genuinely like to hear from you. Community at **novawerk.io**. Source at **github.com/Novawerk/YIMA**.

Thanks for reading. Hope you find it useful.
